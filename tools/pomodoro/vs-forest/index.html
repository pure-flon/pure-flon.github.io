When Forest App Is the Better Choice

You need phone-locking to prevent distraction. Forest's signature feature — locking your phone screen during a session — is a powerful intervention for people who habitually check their phone. A browser timer can't do this. If your main enemy is your phone, Forest's lock feature is genuinely useful.

Gamification keeps you motivated. Growing a virtual forest, collecting tree species, and contributing to real-world tree planting resonates deeply with some users — especially students. If visual rewards and environmental impact keep you engaged, Forest's gamification may be worth the cost.

You want detailed historical statistics. Forest tracks months of focus data with daily/weekly charts, streak calendars, and productivity patterns. If long-term tracking matters to you, Forest's data features are more comprehensive than our timer's session counters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Forest app free?

Forest is $1.99 on iOS. On Android it's free with ads; a paid version removes ads. The Chrome extension is free with limited features. Premium subscriptions unlock advanced statistics and more tree species. pure-flon Pomodoro Timer has no paid tier — it's free in all browsers with no ads.

Can I use a Pomodoro timer without installing an app?

Yes. pure-flon Pomodoro Timer runs at pure-flon.com/tools/pomodoro/ in any modern browser. No install, no account. On mobile, you can add it to your home screen for app-like access (Safari on iOS: Share → Add to Home Screen; Chrome on Android: Menu → Add to Home Screen).

Does Forest actually plant real trees?

Yes. Forest partners with Trees for the Future (trees.org). Users earn virtual coins by completing focus sessions, and those coins can be spent to have Forest make a real-world donation for tree planting. It's a genuine component of the app, independently verified by the nonprofit partner.

What is the best free alternative to Forest App?

For a free, browser-based alternative without gamification, pure-flon Pomodoro Timer is a strong choice. For web-based alternatives with task tracking, Pomofocus.io is worth trying. For macOS/iOS users who want a free native app, Be Focused has a generous free tier.
