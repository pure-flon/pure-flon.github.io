Monthly Compound Interest Formula

A = P × (1 + r/12)^(12t) + M × [(1 + r/12)^(12t) − 1] / (r/12) P = principal · r = annual rate (decimal) · t = years · M = monthly contribution

Example: $10,000 principal + $200/month at 7% for 10 years → Final value ≈ $47,861. The $10,000 grew to $20,097 on its own; the $24,000 in contributions ($200 × 120 months) became $34,783 — the extra $10,783 is pure compound interest on contributions.

Monthly Compound Interest — FAQ

What is the formula for monthly compound interest?

A = P(1 + r/12)^(12t), where A = final amount, P = principal, r = annual rate as decimal, t = years. At 7% annual, $10,000 compounded monthly for 10 years: A = 10,000 × (1.07/12 + 1)^120 = $20,097. With $200/month additions: use A = P(1+r/12)^(12t) + M×[(1+r/12)^(12t)−1]/(r/12).

How much more does monthly compounding earn vs annual?

At 7% on $10,000 over 30 years: annual = $76,123; monthly = $81,165. That's $5,042 extra — a 6.6% bonus — just from compounding more frequently. The gap widens at higher interest rates (at 12%, monthly compounding earns ~20% more than annual over 30 years).

How do monthly contributions affect compound interest?

Adding even $100/month transforms returns dramatically. Starting with $5,000 at 7%, adding $100/month for 20 years: final value ≈ $63,400 vs $19,350 without contributions. The extra $24,000 contributed generates $19,750 in compound interest — almost doubling the contribution value through compounding.

Which accounts compound monthly?

Most savings accounts, high-yield savings (HY savings), money market accounts, and many CDs compound monthly. Mortgages in the US also compound monthly. Stock market index funds don't "compound" in the traditional sense — returns are generated by price appreciation, but reinvested dividends behave similarly to monthly compounding.

How long to double money with monthly compounding at 7%?

Use the Rule of 72 as a quick estimate: 72 ÷ 7 = 10.3 years. More precisely with monthly compounding, it takes about 10.0 years to double at 7% annual rate. At 10% it takes ~7.0 years; at 5% it takes ~14.0 years. Monthly compounding reduces the doubling time by about 2–3% compared to annual compounding.
