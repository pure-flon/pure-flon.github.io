Crypto Position Sizing Formula

Dollar Risk = Account Balance × Risk % Stop Loss % = |Entry Price − Stop Price| / Entry Price Position Size ($) = Dollar Risk / Stop Loss % Coin Amount = Position Size / Entry Price With leverage: Margin Required = Position Size / Leverage

Example: $10,000 account, 1% risk, BTC entry $65,000, stop $62,700. Dollar risk = $100. Stop loss % = ($65,000 − $62,700) / $65,000 = 3.54%. Position size = $100 / 0.0354 = $2,825. Coin amount = $2,825 / $65,000 = 0.0434 BTC. If BTC hits your stop at $62,700, you lose exactly $100 = 1% of account.

Crypto Position Sizing — FAQ

Why is position sizing critical in crypto?

Crypto is 3-5× more volatile than stocks. Bitcoin has historically dropped 80-90% from peaks (2018: -84%, 2022: -77%). Without proper position sizing, a single leveraged trade can wipe 20-50% of your account. Professional traders who survive long-term almost universally use fixed-% risk rules. The math: at 2% risk/trade with 50% win rate and 2:1 R:R, you'll profit long-term regardless of which individual trades win or lose.

How do I set a stop loss for Bitcoin?

BTC stop loss placement: (1) Below key support levels (swing lows, major moving averages). (2) Below ATR-based levels: BTC's daily ATR is typically $1,500-3,000, so stops under 1× ATR often get stopped out by normal volatility. (3) As a percentage: 3-8% stops for swing trades, 1-2% for intraday scalps. Wider stops require smaller position sizes to maintain the same dollar risk. Never use round-number stops — set them 0.5-1% beyond the obvious level to avoid stop-hunting.

What is a good R:R ratio for crypto trading?

Minimum 2:1 risk-reward (R:R) for any crypto trade. This means your take profit is at least 2× further from entry than your stop loss. At 2:1 R:R with 50% win rate, you break even after fees. At 3:1 with 40% win rate, you're profitable. Crypto trend trades often offer 5:1 to 10:1 R:R during breakouts. Avoid "revenge trades" with 1:1 or worse R:R — they're mathematically guaranteed to lose long-term unless you win over 55%+ of trades.

Should I use leverage for crypto trading?

Only if experienced. Statistics: 75-85% of leveraged crypto traders lose money. 10× leverage means a 10% adverse move liquidates your position. With Bitcoin's 3-5% daily volatility, 10× leverage is extremely risky. If you use leverage: start at 2-3×, reduce position size (half your normal $ risk), use tight stops, and never use more than 10-20% of your account as margin. Spot trading crypto already provides high returns during bull markets without liquidation risk.
