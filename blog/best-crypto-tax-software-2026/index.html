How to Choose the Right Crypto Tax Software

For casual holders (under 100 transactions)

If you only bought and sold crypto on 1-2 exchanges and have under 100 transactions, almost any tool works. Start with CoinTracker's free tier (25 txns) to see if it covers you. If not, Koinly's $49 Hodler plan is the safest choice — it covers 100 transactions and produces IRS Form 8949 directly.

For active traders (100–5,000 transactions)

Koinly Trader ($99/yr) or CryptoTaxCalculator Investor ($99/yr) are the sweet spots. Both handle 1,000 transactions and have robust exchange API support. If you trade primarily on US exchanges, Koinly's TurboTax export is seamless. If you're outside the US, CryptoTaxCalculator supports your local tax rules.

For DeFi users

DeFi is where most crypto tax software fails. The core challenge: every liquidity pool action, swap, and yield harvest creates multiple taxable events. CryptoTaxCalculator and ZenLedger lead here. Koinly handles common protocols (Uniswap, Aave) well but may miscategorize complex multi-hop transactions.

Pro tip: Import the same transactions into 2 tools and compare totals. If they differ by more than 1-2%, investigate why — one tool may be miscategorizing a transaction type. This discrepancy review saved some users thousands in over-reported gains.

Tax loss harvesting: which tools support it?

All major tools show unrealized losses. TaxBit and ZenLedger have dedicated harvesting dashboards that show exactly which positions to sell before year-end to maximize tax savings. Koinly shows it in the dashboard but doesn't calculate the optimal sell amounts. For high-net-worth crypto holders, this feature alone can justify the software cost many times over.

Crypto Tax Software — FAQ

Is Koinly the best crypto tax software?

For most traders, yes. Koinly leads in exchange coverage (750+), handles DeFi reasonably well, and produces US-standard tax forms (Form 8949, Schedule D). The $99/yr Trader plan covers 1,000 transactions, which handles most individual investors. The main limitation: very complex DeFi (LP positions, multi-protocol yield strategies) sometimes requires manual review and correction.

Do I have to pay taxes on crypto?

Yes, in the US. The IRS taxes crypto as property (Notice 2014-21). Taxable events: selling crypto for fiat, swapping one crypto for another, paying for goods/services with crypto, receiving staking/mining rewards. NOT taxable: buying crypto, holding crypto, transferring between your own wallets. Short-term gains (under 1 year): taxed as ordinary income (10-37%). Long-term gains (over 1 year): 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income.

What is the cheapest crypto tax software?

Free options: CoinTracker (25 txns), ZenLedger (25 txns), Accointing (25 txns). For paid: Koinly Hodler $49/yr (100 txns) or ZenLedger Starter $49/yr. If you have very few transactions (under 25), use the free tiers. For US users with under 10 trades, your exchange's built-in tax report (Coinbase, Binance) may be sufficient for Schedule D, but most need dedicated software for accuracy.

Can the IRS track crypto?

Yes. Since 2023, major US exchanges (Coinbase, Kraken, Gemini) report to the IRS via 1099-B/1099-DA forms. Starting 2025-2026, brokers must report customer transactions under new IRS regulations. The IRS also uses blockchain analytics tools (Chainalysis, CipherTrace) to identify unreported wallets. The safest approach: report all crypto transactions accurately — the cost of a good tax tool ($50-200/yr) is minimal compared to audit risk.
